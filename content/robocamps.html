About

At RoboCamps, elementary and middle school students, ages 8-14, will have fun working hands-on to construct a competitive robot while learning and developing engineering and computer skills.

Over the course of a week, campers will work in small groups to design, build, and program their own robot using materials from the VEX IQ Ecosystem. With the help of our counselors, campers will develop and apply technical skills as well as teamwork and sportsmanship in a friendly competition.

Four sessions of RoboCamps will be offered in total, with each week featuring a different game. Read further to learn more about the material being taught in each of these weeks.

Camp Info

These sessions will allow campers to build and program a basic robot using parts from a VEX IQ kit. Campers will learn about block code, sensors, and more to enable their robot to complete a challenge. At the end of the week, campers will participate in a virtual competition!

  • Monday: Begin building the base robot!
  • Tuesday: Begin coding, complete coding challenges, and talk about possible strategies for the game.
  • Wednesday: Complete a line-following challenge and discuss sensors to implement into your robot.
  • Thursday: Discuss gear ratios and complete a gear ratio challenge. Finish up working on your robot.
  • Friday: Put your robot to the test by completing autonomous and teleoperated challenges for a specific VEX IQ game!

Week 1: Ringmaster (7/11 - 7/15)

Attain the highest score by Scoring Rings on the Floor Goal and on Posts, by having Uniform Posts, by Emptying Starting Pegs, and by Releasing the Bonus Tray.


Week 2: Rise Above (7/18 - 7/22)

Attain the highest score by Scoring and Stacking Risers in Goals, Completing Rows, and Completing Stacks.


Week 3: High Rise (7/25 - 7/29)

Bring the red, green, and blue Scoring Cubes from their starting position to the Scoring Zone and by building Highrises on each color’s Highrise Base.


Week 4: Squared Away (8/1 - 8/5)

Attain the highest score by Scoring Balls in or on Cubes, and Scoring Cubes in Corner Goals or on Platforms.

FAQ

Q: Who are the camp instructors?

A: The camp instructors will consist of student members and adult mentors from Team 4099. All instructors have mastery over the content they are teaching.

Q: How is this different from last year's online RoboCamps?

A: RoboCamps will now be held in-person, and campers will build and program their robots in a classroom facility. Students will no longer need to pick up kits or attend a Zoom meeting from home. We plan to host the sessions at Hallie Wells (TEMP) Middle School (11701 Little Seneca Pkwy, Clarksburg, MD 20871), and we will send further information once available.

Q: Who are RoboCamps meant for?

A: RoboCamps are meant for older elementary and middle school students who want to get started with robotics, design, and/or programming.

Q: My child has no experience with robotics, or programming. Can they still participate?

Yes! Our camp is geared towards students of all experience levels. There are no specific prerequisites for any of the sessions.

Q: I am interested in attending RoboCamps, but cost is an issue.

A: If the $325 tuition is a barrier to you for registration, please feel free to apply for a full scholarship by emailing this form to robocamps@team4099.com. Team 4099’s goal is to help children and teams learn, regardless of economic circumstances.

Q: What's the difference between the four sessions?

All sessions follow the same structure and give campers access to the same resources, but each session has a different theme. Each game theme has different game pieces and objectives. Campers will strategize and design for robots that are unique to each session’s game. Since each session offers a new competition experience, we welcome campers to sign up for as many sessions as they would like to.

Q: What precautionary steps is Team 4099 taking in regards to Covid?

A: Masks will be expected to be worn at all times during RoboCamps sessions, and we will have extra masks on hand if necessary. Proof of vaccination must be uploaded to the registration form before the camp start date, and regular sanitization will take place before and after each session.

Q: How will lunch work in the context of Covid?

A: There will be a designated time each day for eating lunch. We expect campers to bring their own lunch since we will not be distributing any food. Due to COVID-19, lunch will take place outside as much as possible to allow for social distancing, so please prepare accordingly.

Q: What happens if a camper or counselor tests positive?

A: All parents and guardians will be immediately notified and RoboCamps will transition into an online setting. If your child tests positive, please email robocamps@team4099.com with details of your child’s positive test so we can keep everyone safe.

Q: What if a camper is unable to prove their vaccination status through their card?

A: If you have lost a vaccination card, we encourage you to contact your vaccination provider to obtain a new card. If this is not possible, please contact us, and we will work with you on a case-by-case basis.
